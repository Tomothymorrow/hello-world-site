Esto También Pasará, Mi Hijo

A Letter of Love in the Shadow of a Pandemic

Esto También Pasará, Mi Hijo is a deeply personal, poetic short story born from the chaos of the coronavirus pandemic. Written as a letter from father to son, it captures the raw emotion, uncertainty, and longing for human connection during a time of worldwide isolation.

It's a story about what we miss, what we remember, and what we promise to return to — the small things that become huge when the world goes still: beers and bad movies, laughter and presence, touch and time.

Translated as “This Too Shall Pass, My Son,” this piece has evolved into a multimedia project featuring AI narration, ambient music, and an animated adaptation. But at its core, it’s just one message from a father to his child: “If I go, know that I loved you more than I ever feared the end.”
